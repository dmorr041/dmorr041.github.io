Carrier 360 by J.B. Hunt is an all-in-one solution for transportation carriers of any size. Whether you're a small trucking company with a few trucks and a single Department of Transportation (DOT) number, or a corporation as large as Amazon, you can sign up with our platform and receive access to our loadboard.

We integrate directly with Dial-a-Truck (DAT), and offer competitive rates and a full auctioning system for our loads. Users can place competitive bids on loads, book loads at fixed pricing models, track their loads from the tendering process through the delivery of the load, and even handle payments within our app.

One of the more notable features I worked on was integrating Google Maps into the app, so carriers and drivers can more accurately see the loads near them, and any pit stops they might need along the way. We also have a scoring system for loads measuring things like delivery time, update frequency, and other transit related metrics, as well as SMS, email, in-app and push notifications for reminders and alerts on significant events during the freight lifecycle.

The application is built using React Native for mobile devices and Angular for the web. We use a containerized microservices infrastructure server-side, written in Spring Framework and Java. We manage our pipelines via Azure, and our Kubernetes resources are deployed top-nav GKE Clusters. We also come complete with a full eventing architecture utilizing both AMQ and Kafka.

Open Research is a Google Chrome Extension that integrates the Open AI Chat Completions API to make research more readable and understandable to the average layman. Highlight text you would like to better understand in the browser, then simply right-click to open the context menu and hit translate and a GPT interpreation of what you are reading becomes available in the sidebar.

This project was built because of a common problem my friends and I faced during 2020 - we all found ourselves reading more reasearch papers, particularly around Covid, but most of us didn't have a background in research or in medicine, so digesting and understanding that material proved difficult.

It's built using the Google Chrome Extension APIs, which utilize plain old HTML, CSS, and browser-based Javascript.
