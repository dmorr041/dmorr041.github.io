Carrier 360 by J.B. Hunt is a complete solution for transportation carriers of any size. Whether you're a small trucking company with a few trucks and a single Department of Transportation (DOT) number, or a corporation as large as Amazon, you can sign up with our platform and receive access to our loadboard.

We integrate directly with Dial-a-Truck (DAT), and offer competitive rates and a full auctioning system for our loads. Users can place competitive bids on loads, book loads at fixed pricing models, track their loads from the tendering process, through the delivery of the load and even handle payments within our app.

I had the pleasure of integrating Google Maps into the app, so carriers and drivers can more accurately see the loads near them, and any pit stops they might need along the way. We also have a scoring system for loads measuring things like delivery time, update frequency, and other transit related metrics, as well as SMS, email, in-app and push notifications for reminders and alerts on significant events during the freight lifecycle.

The application is built using React Native for Mobile devices and Angular on the web. We use a containerized microservices infrastructure server-side, written in Spring Framework and Java. Our Kubernetes resources are managed and deployed via GKE Clusters, and we come complete with a full eventing architecture utilizing both AMQ and Kafka.
